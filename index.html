PRODUCTIVE THINGS TO DO WHILE AT HOME (OR) IN QUARANTINE.

You’re at home or in quarantine and need to keep yourself active and productive. For many, this lock down period has given more time on our hands than we have ever had. Time which we never thought we would have. Being in quarantine is a new experience and our mental health care can be declining. We have never known a life of strictly working from home, only going out for groceries, and not being able to visit family and friends.

Concerts are postponed, Sporting events are cancelled, Schools are closed and tourists hotspots are shut down, experts recommend that even those who show no sign of illness, to stay home during this time global pandemic. So what is there to do when you’re sitting at home, peeping through your window, thinking about life that once was. Scrolling through social media is not great for your mental health. So as we adjust to this new reality, here are a few ideas of what you can do to keep busy and keep routine while you’re under quarantine.

This post reviews a number of productive activities which you can understand.

READING BOOKS

First on this list, is very much where you should be starting. Books exercise the brain, build our vocabularies, lower stress and also enhances the imagination. Reading makes you more empathetic. Reading is a way to escape your own life, can take you to faraway lands, other times, and put you in other people’s shoes. Reading reduces stress. Learning to read is about listening and understanding as well as working out what is printed on the page. Thought hearing stories, children are exposed to a wide range of words. This helps them build. Their own vocabulary and improve their understanding when they listen, which is vital as they start to read.

COOKING

Involving your children in food preparation (may be by asking them to read the recipe out loud or mix ingredients) is not only a fun thing to do, but also a great way to teach them healthy eating habits.

Good foods to eat during the COVID19 pandemic:

You should eat a variety of fresh and unprocessed foods every day to get the vitamins, minerals, dietary fibre, protein and antioxidants your body needs. Drink enough water eat fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts and whole grains. For snacks, choose raw vegetables and fresh fruits, rather than foods that are high in sugar, fat (or) salt.

GET ARTISTIC

Art opens the heart and mind to possibilities and fuels the imagination. Art is a process of learning to create ourselves and experience the world in new ways. It supports the bigger view of life: beauty, symbols, spirituality of storytelling and allows us to be present in the moment. Art keeps the magic alive.

ENROL A FREE COURSE

Enrolling on a course and successfully passing tests and assignments will help build up your knowledge-base as well as your CV. A free profile on the open learn website gives you access to your personal learning profile and record of achievements that you earn while you study. There are also a range other courses you can learn from.

LERAN NEW SKILL

Building up your qualifications by enrolling in courses can also be accompanied by learning new skills. This could be learning the rules of a game (or) how to play an instrument. I think can be good fun, and its also an inventive way to keep yourself entertained and constantly learning.

SET UP WORKOUT ROUTINE

Workout routine is important in these quarantine days, we eat much staying at home without workout. If you start workout it helps you control your weight. Reduces your risk of heart disease. Helps your body manage blood sugar and insulin levels. Improves your mental health and mood.

YOGA AND MEDITATION

Yoga’s incorporation of meditation and breathing can help improve a persons mental well being “regular yoga practice creates mental clarity and calmness; increases body awareness; relieves chronic stress patterns; relaxes the mind; centres attention; and sharpens concentration.

KEEP IN TOUCH WITH FAMILY, FRIENDS AND PAST COLLEGUES

We’re isolated from people we usually spend time with but we can be connected with them via more means more than even before. Make time to catch up and have a virtual drink with you friends and family. I also feel that reconnecting with past colleagues is a great idea. You would have spent a lot of time with these people in previous roles and might have grown apart as you move on your career.

LISTEN TO MORE MUSIC

Improves mood. Studies show that listening to music can benefit overall well. Being, help regulate emotion, and create happiness and relaxation in everyday life. It reduces stress, improves memory, lessens anxiety.

LEARN A NEW LANGUAGE

How many times have you said that you’d like to learn a new language? I’m sure you have many time to do so. With apps like MEMRISE and DUOLINGO, you can do so easily and no cost.

Stick to your routine and form the habits to keep your working and keep you productive during this lockdown.
